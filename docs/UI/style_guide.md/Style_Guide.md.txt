🎨 Core Color System

  Primary Deep Navy       Science Teal         Atomic Cyan        Energy Amber
     [ #0F1E36 ]          [ #008080 ]          [ #00E5FF ]         [ #FFB300 ]

1. Primary Colors (Brand Identity & Structure)
These colors form the skeletal structure of the app, establishing a trustworthy, academic, and highly professional scientific environment.
• Primary Deep Navy (#0F1E36):
  • Concept: Represents deep space, molecular structures, and ultimate authority.

  • Usage: App headers, core navigation bars, primary text, and structural container boundaries.

  • Psychology: Trustworthy, stable, and intellectual.

• Neutral Slate (#2D3748):
  • Concept: A balanced neutral gray.

  • Usage: Primary body text, descriptions, and textbook passages. It is much softer on the eyes than pure #000000.

2. Secondary Colors (Functional Division)
These colors draw attention to active interactive elements, distinct chemistry modules (e.g., periodic table trends vs. equation solvers), and highlighted concepts.
• Science Teal (#008080):
  • Concept: The color of classical laboratory glass and chemical solutions.

  • Usage: Primary buttons, active tab indicators, and featured cards.

  • Psychology: Analytical, calm, and scientific.

• Soft Ether Blue (#E6FFFA):
  • Concept: A highly diluted, clean background tint.

  • Usage: Light mode card backgrounds, subtle text highlights, and info-banners.

3. Accent Colors (Focal Points)
Spurring interest and spotlighting critical interactions or gamified milestones (like matching formulas or completing a chemistry deck).
• Atomic Cyan (#00E5FF):
  • Concept: High-energy chemical bonding and modern UI glow.

  • Usage: Focus rings, progress meters, active molecular bond highlights, and interactive node selections.

• Energy Amber (#FFB300):
  • Concept: Representing electrons, heat, and reactions.

  • Usage: Premium feature badges, daily streaks, achievements, and structural warnings.

4. System Feedback & Chemistry Safety Colors
An educational chemistry app requires a strict feedback system to guide students through right/wrong answers and alert them to toxic/safe compounds in laboratory modules.
• Safe Green (#10B981): Correct answers in quizzes, neutral/safe pH levels, and stable elements.

• Warning Orange (#F59E0B): Intermediate quiz hints, unstable isotopic states, and moderate hazard warnings.

• Hazard Red (#EF4444): Incorrect answers, explosive/toxic compound warnings (GHS symbols), and critical UI errors.

To support student study patterns—which shift from bright classrooms during the day to late-night cramming in dorm rooms—the palette seamlessly flips background/foreground values while maintaining identical semantic meaning.

♿ Accessibility & Contrast Compliance (WCAG 2.1)
To ensure this educational system is accessible to all students, including those with visual impairments or color vision deficiencies (color-blindness), the color system employs strict accessibility rules:
1. Strict Contrast Ratios (AA & AAA Standards):
  • In Light Mode, any body text (#2D3748) on a white surface (#FFFFFF) easily exceeds the 4.5:1 WCAG AA requirement, reaching a highly readable contrast of over 10:1.

  • In Dark Mode, neon interactive text/labels are boosted. For example, instead of dark Science Teal (#008080), interactive text switches to Neon Teal (#4FD1C5) on dark surfaces (#121B2D) to ensure it stays above 4.5:1.

2. No "Color-Only" States (The Deuteranopia / Protanopia Shield):
  • Rule: Never rely solely on red/green to indicate right/wrong quiz answers or hazards.

  • Implementation: Combine colors with Icons and Micro-copy. A correct quiz choice displays the Safe Green (#10B981) color alongside a checkmark icon ($\checkmark$) and the word "Correct!". An error displays Hazard Red (#EF4444) with a cross icon ($\times$).

3. Visual Weight Hierarchy:
  • Text inputs always feature a clear focus ring in Atomic Cyan (#00E5FF) with a 2px width to make keyboard navigation obvious for users with limited mobility or vision.
