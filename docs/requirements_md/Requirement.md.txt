## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the functional and non-functional requirements for **Chemistry AI**, an artificial intelligence–powered platform designed to assist students, educators, and researchers in learning, teaching, and applying chemistry concepts. The document serves as a reference for the design, development, testing, and deployment teams, and as an agreement between stakeholders on the scope of the system.

### 1.2 Scope
Chemistry AI is a web and mobile-accessible application that uses AI/ML models (including large language models, computer vision, and chemistry-specific computational engines) to:
- Answer chemistry questions and explain concepts in natural language.
- Balance chemical equations and solve stoichiometry problems.
- Recognize and interpret chemical structures from text, images, or drawings.
- Predict reaction products and mechanisms for common organic and inorganic reactions.
- Generate practice problems, quizzes, and personalized study plans.
- Provide lab safety guidance and virtual lab simulations.
- Support periodic table exploration, molecular visualization (2D/3D), and unit conversions.

The system is intended for use in secondary education, higher education, and self-learners, with an extensible architecture allowing future integration into institutional Learning Management Systems (LMS).

### 1.3 Intended Audience
- Product Managers and Business Analysts
- Software Architects and Developers
- QA/Test Engineers
- UI/UX Designers
- DevOps and Security Engineers
- Academic Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)

## 2. Overall Description

### 2.1 Product Perspective
Chemistry AI is a new, standalone product with optional integrations into third-party LMS platforms (Google Classroom, Moodle, Canvas) and scientific databases (PubChem, ChemSpider). It comprises:
- A **web application** (responsive, browser-based).
- **Mobile applications** (Android/iOS).
- A **backend AI engine** combining an LLM-based reasoning layer with deterministic chemistry computation modules (equation balancers, molecular property calculators).
- A **cloud-hosted database** for user data, content, and analytics.

### 2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- Conversational chemistry tutoring and Q&A
- Chemical equation balancing and stoichiometric calculations
- Molecular structure recognition, drawing, and 3D visualization
- Reaction prediction and mechanism explanation
- Practice test/quiz generation with adaptive difficulty
- Virtual lab simulation and safety guidance
- Progress tracking, analytics, and reporting for students and teachers
- Content library (periodic table, formulas, reference sheets)

### 2.3 User Characteristics
Users range from middle/high school students with limited technical proficiency to university researchers with advanced domain expertise. The system must therefore support adjustable complexity levels in explanations and outputs.

### 2.4 Operating Environment
- Web: Modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge — latest two major versions).
- Mobile: Android 10+, iOS 15+.
- Backend: Cloud-native deployment (containerized microservices), horizontally scalable.

### 2.5 Design and Implementation Constraints
- Must comply with data privacy regulations relevant to the deployment region (e.g., GDPR, COPPA for minors).
- AI-generated content must be factually validated against a curated chemistry knowledge base to reduce hallucination risk.
- Must support offline-limited mode for core reference features (periodic table, formulas) in mobile apps.

### 2.6 Assumptions and Dependencies
- Users have basic internet connectivity for AI-powered features.
- Third-party chemical databases (e.g., PubChem) remain accessible via API.
- Institutional users provide valid organizational credentials for SSO integration.

## 4. Functional Requirements

### 4.1 User Account Management
- **FR-1.1**: The system shall allow users to register via email, phone number, or third-party SSO (Google, Microsoft, Apple).
- **FR-1.2**: The system shall support role assignment (Student, Teacher, Admin, etc.) during or after registration.
- **FR-1.3**: The system shall allow users to reset passwords via secure verification (email/OTP).
- **FR-1.4**: The system shall support MFA for account login.
- **FR-1.5**: Institution Admins shall be able to bulk-create and manage student/teacher accounts via CSV import or LMS sync.

### 4.2 AI Chemistry Q&A and Tutoring
- **FR-2.1**: The system shall accept natural language questions (text and voice) related to chemistry topics.
- **FR-2.2**: The system shall generate step-by-step explanations, adjustable to the user's selected education level (middle school, high school, undergraduate, advanced).
- **FR-2.3**: The system shall cite or reference verified sources for factual claims (e.g., periodic data, known reaction outcomes).
- **FR-2.4**: The system shall flag and route uncertain or potentially inaccurate AI responses for human/content moderator review.
- **FR-2.5**: The system shall support multi-turn conversational context (follow-up questions).

### 4.3 Equation Balancing & Stoichiometry
- **FR-3.1**: The system shall balance chemical equations entered in text or image form.
- **FR-3.2**: The system shall perform stoichiometric calculations (moles, mass, limiting reagent, percent yield) given user inputs.
- **FR-3.3**: The system shall display balanced equations with step-by-step derivation.
- **FR-3.4**: The system shall validate equations for chemical plausibility (e.g., valid elements, charge balance).

### 4.4 Molecular Structure Recognition & Visualization
- **FR-4.1**: The system shall accept molecular structures via SMILES notation, file upload (e.g., MOL/SDF), or hand-drawn image input (via OCR/structure recognition).
- **FR-4.2**: The system shall render 2D and 3D visualizations of molecular structures.
- **FR-4.3**: The system shall calculate and display molecular properties (molar mass, functional groups, IUPAC name where feasible).
- **FR-4.4**: The system shall allow users to export molecular visualizations as image files or standard chemical formats.

### 4.5 Reaction Prediction & Mechanisms
- **FR-5.1**: The system shall predict likely products for common organic and inorganic reactions given reactants and conditions.
- **FR-5.2**: The system shall display reaction mechanisms with electron-pushing arrows for organic reactions where applicable.
- **FR-5.3**: The system shall indicate confidence level or known limitations for predicted reactions (e.g., "well-established" vs. "AI-estimated").

### 4.6 Practice Problems, Quizzes & Assessments
- **FR-6.1**: The system shall generate practice questions based on selected topics and difficulty levels.
- **FR-6.2**: The system shall adapt question difficulty based on user performance (adaptive learning).
- **FR-6.3**: Teachers shall be able to create, edit, and assign custom quizzes to specific students or classes.
- **FR-6.4**: The system shall auto-grade objective questions and provide AI-assisted grading suggestions for subjective answers.
- **FR-6.5**: The system shall generate performance reports per student, per class, and per topic.

### 4.7 Virtual Labs & Safety Guidance
- **FR-7.1**: The system shall provide simulated virtual lab experiments for common chemistry procedures (titration, distillation, etc.).
- **FR-7.2**: The system shall provide safety data sheet (SDS) summaries and hazard warnings for chemicals referenced by users.
- **FR-7.3**: The system shall alert users when a queried combination of chemicals/procedures poses a known safety risk.

### 4.8 Content Library & Reference Tools
- **FR-8.1**: The system shall provide an interactive periodic table with element details.
- **FR-8.2**: The system shall provide unit conversion tools (concentration, pressure, temperature, etc.).
- **FR-8.3**: The system shall provide a searchable glossary of chemistry terms and formulas.

### 4.9 Progress Tracking & Reporting
- **FR-9.1**: The system shall track individual student progress across topics and time.
- **FR-9.2**: The system shall generate downloadable/exportable progress reports (PDF/CSV).
- **FR-9.3**: Teachers and Institution Admins shall have dashboard views of aggregated class/institution performance.

### 4.10 Notifications & Communication
- **FR-10.1**: The system shall send notifications for assigned quizzes, deadlines, and grades.
- **FR-10.2**: The system shall support in-app announcements from teachers to their classes.

### 4.11 Integrations
- **FR-11.1**: The system shall support integration with major LMS platforms via LTI (Learning Tools Interoperability) standard.
- **FR-11.2**: The system shall provide a documented REST API for third-party or institutional integrations (tiered access).

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements

### 5.1 Usability
- **NFR-1.1**: The UI shall follow WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility guidelines.
- **NFR-1.2**: The system shall support multiple languages (initial release: English, with roadmap for additional languages).
- **NFR-1.3**: New users shall be able to complete first meaningful interaction (e.g., ask a question, view a molecule) within 2 minutes of onboarding, without external help.

### 5.2 Reliability & Availability
- **NFR-2.1**: The system shall maintain 99.5% uptime, measured monthly, excluding scheduled maintenance windows.
- **NFR-2.2**: The system shall implement automated failover for critical services (AI inference, authentication).
- **NFR-2.3**: Data backups shall occur at least once every 24 hours with point-in-time recovery capability.

### 5.3 Maintainability
- **NFR-3.1**: The system shall be built on a modular microservices architecture to allow independent updates to AI models and feature modules.
- **NFR-3.2**: The AI knowledge base shall be updatable without requiring full system redeployment.

### 5.4 Portability
- **NFR-4.1**: The web application shall function consistently across supported browsers and screen sizes (responsive design).
- **NFR-4.2**: Mobile applications shall maintain feature parity with the web application, where hardware permits.

### 5.5 Compliance
- **NFR-5.1**: The system shall comply with GDPR, and where applicable, COPPA/FERPA for users under 18 or in educational institutional contexts.
- **NFR-5.2**: The system shall maintain audit logs for data access and administrative actions for at least 12 months.

### 5.6 Scalability
- **NFR-6.1**: The system shall support horizontal scaling to accommodate peak concurrent usage (e.g., exam periods) without service degradation.
- **NFR-6.2**: The architecture shall support at least 100,000 concurrent active users at general availability, with a scaling roadmap beyond that.

## 7. Security Requirements

### 7.1 Authentication & Authorization
- **SEC-1.1**: All user authentication shall use industry-standard protocols (OAuth 2.0 / OpenID Connect).
- **SEC-1.2**: The system shall enforce Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) across all modules.
- **SEC-1.3**: MFA shall be mandatory for Teacher, Admin, and System Administrator roles, and optional (but encouraged) for Students.
- **SEC-1.4**: Session tokens shall expire after a configurable period of inactivity (default: 30 minutes for web, 14 days for mobile with refresh tokens).

### 7.2 Data Protection
- **SEC-2.1**: All data in transit shall be encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
- **SEC-2.2**: All sensitive data at rest (PII, credentials, institutional records) shall be encrypted using AES-256 or equivalent.
- **SEC-2.3**: Passwords shall be stored using a strong salted hashing algorithm (e.g., bcrypt/Argon2); plaintext storage is prohibited.
- **SEC-2.4**: PII shall be minimized in AI model logs; conversation logs used for model improvement shall be anonymized or pseudonymized.

### 7.3 Application Security
- **SEC-3.1**: The system shall be developed following OWASP Top 10 mitigation practices (injection prevention, secure input validation, etc.).
- **SEC-3.2**: The system shall undergo periodic third-party penetration testing (at least annually).
- **SEC-3.3**: File uploads (e.g., molecule images) shall be scanned and validated to prevent malicious payloads.
- **SEC-3.4**: Rate limiting and abuse detection shall be applied to AI query endpoints to prevent misuse (e.g., attempts to extract harmful chemical synthesis information).

### 7.4 Content Safety
- **SEC-4.1**: The AI engine shall include safeguards to prevent generation of instructions for creating hazardous, illegal, or dangerous substances, regardless of user framing (educational, fictional, etc.).
- **SEC-4.2**: The system shall log and review flagged high-risk queries in accordance with a documented safety policy.

### 7.5 Data Privacy & Governance
- **SEC-5.1**: Users shall be able to request export or deletion of their personal data in compliance with applicable data protection laws.
- **SEC-5.2**: Data belonging to minors shall be subject to additional access restrictions and parental/guardian consent workflows where legally required.
- **SEC-5.3**: Institution data shall be logically isolated (multi-tenancy) to prevent cross-institution data leakage.

## 8. Performance Requirements

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| AI Q&A response time (text query) | ≤ 3 seconds (p95) |
| Equation balancing / stoichiometry calculation | ≤ 1 second (p95) |
| Molecular structure rendering (2D) | ≤ 2 seconds (p95) |
| Molecular structure rendering (3D) | ≤ 4 seconds (p95) |
| Image-based structure recognition (OCR) | ≤ 5 seconds (p95) |
| Quiz auto-grading (objective questions) | ≤ 2 seconds per submission |
| Dashboard/report load time | ≤ 3 seconds (p95) |
| System availability | ≥ 99.5% monthly uptime |
| Concurrent user support (GA target) | 100,000+ concurrent sessions |
| API throughput (institutional tier) | ≥ 500 requests/second sustained |

Performance shall be continuously monitored via application performance monitoring (APM) tooling, with automated alerting for SLA breaches.

## 9. System Interfaces

### 9.1 User Interfaces
- Responsive web application (desktop, tablet, mobile browsers).
- Native mobile applications (Android, iOS).

### 9.2 Hardware Interfaces
- No specialized hardware required; standard consumer devices with camera access (for structure image input) and microphone access (for voice queries) recommended.

### 9.3 Software Interfaces
- Integration with LMS platforms via LTI 1.3.
- Integration with chemical databases (e.g., PubChem, ChemSpider) via REST APIs.
- Integration with cloud identity providers for SSO.

### 9.4 Communication Interfaces
- HTTPS/REST for all client-server communication.
- WebSocket support for real-time conversational AI interactions.

---

## 10. Assumptions and Constraints
- The AI reasoning layer relies on a hybrid approach: LLM-based natural language understanding combined with deterministic chemistry engines for calculations, to minimize factual errors in numeric/structural outputs.
- Full offline functionality is out of scope for v1.0; only limited offline reference tools are supported.
- Real-time collaborative lab sessions (multi-user virtual labs) are out of scope for v1.0.
- The system depends on continued availability and licensing terms of third-party chemical databases and AI model providers.

## 11. Future Scope
- **Multi-language expansion**: Support for regional languages beyond initial release (e.g., Hindi, Bengali, Spanish, Mandarin).
- **AR/VR Lab Simulations**: Immersive virtual reality lab experiments for deeper experiential learning.
- **Collaborative Learning**: Multi-user real-time virtual labs and study groups.
- **Advanced Research Tools**: Integration with computational chemistry engines (DFT, molecular dynamics) for university/research-tier users.
- **Handwriting-to-Structure AI**: Improved recognition of complex hand-drawn organic structures and reaction schemes.
- **Offline-First Mobile Mode**: Expanded offline capability for low-connectivity regions.
- **Institutional Analytics Suite**: Predictive analytics for identifying at-risk students based on engagement and performance trends.
- **Voice-First Tutoring Mode**: Fully voice-driven tutoring experience for accessibility and hands-free lab use.
- **Gamification**: Badges, leaderboards, and achievement systems to boost engagement.
- **Plugin Ecosystem**: Third-party developer plugins/extensions via a public API marketplace.
